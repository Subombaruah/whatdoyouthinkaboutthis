Should Innocent People Suffer?

In a world filled with political tension and violence, one question echoes louder than ever — should innocent people suffer for the sins of others?

The Case of Pakistan and India

When terrorism strikes, the world demands justice. But too often, that justice ends up punishing the everyday citizens — the shopkeepers, the students, the workers — who had nothing to do with it.

As tensions rise between India and Pakistan, it's important to remember that the people are not the politics. A terrorist act may come from a group, but entire populations are blamed. This is wrong. This is dangerous.

Is This True Justice?

If a stranger commits a crime in your city, would you want your family punished for it? Then why do we accept it when countries do the same?

A Call for Humanity

This article isn't about taking sides. It's about taking a stand — for common people. For peace. For a world where the innocent aren't crushed in the fight between powers.

“Justice is not served by punishing the voiceless.”

Think about it. Speak up. Share this. Let’s not stay silent when the price of war is paid by those who never signed up for it.
